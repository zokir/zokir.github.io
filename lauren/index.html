Mission

To combine yoga with direct, systematic Orton-Gillingham reading instruction to improve educational outcomes for students in grades K-12.

Philosophy

All children can learn and succeed. Learning best takes place with a mutli-sensory approach, systematic direct instruction, and executive functioning support.

About

As a classroom special education teacher since 2004, I have a strong background in teaching struggling readers. After becoming certified as a yoga instructor in 2020, it quickly dawned on me that yoga contains a missing piece in combating the limitations of the school day in a typical classroom. Reading instruction is routinely delivered in a stressful environment for the struggling learner. However, mindfulness, movement, and mental preparedness through yoga therapy techniques teaches the struggling reader to lean into the discomfort of learning with a disability or difference. With tools for a calm and open mental state combined with a systematic, multi-sensory reading program, the learning process is accelerated. Yoga can address difficulties associated with reading anxiety and frustration, as well as neurodiversity such as ADHD and autism. Yoga breathing techniques and poses are interspersed throughout each tutoring session. This creates a routine for each student to prepare for learning, ease anxiety while practicing reading skills, and end each session on a positive note acknowledging the progress that has been made. Yoga strategies can be transferred to the home and classroom settings, improving outcomes for the learner as a whole.
