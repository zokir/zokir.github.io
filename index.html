About

Orton-Gillingham Tutor, Mindfulness Coach & Special Educator

As a classroom special education teacher for 10 years, I have a strong background in teaching struggling readers. As a fun side project I became certified as a yoga instructor in 2020. It quickly dawned on me that yoga contains a missing piece in combating the limitations and constraints of the school day in a typical classroom. Reading instruction is routinely delivered in isolation and in a stressful environment for the struggling learner. Mindfulness, movement, and mental preparedness through yoga teaches the struggling reader to lean into the discomfort of learning with a disability or difference. With tools for a calm and open mental state combined with a systematic, multi-sensory reading program, the learning process is accelerated. Yoga can address difficulties associated with reading anxiety and frustration, as well as neurodiversity to include ADHD and autism. Yoga breathing techniques and poses are interspersed throughout each tutoring session. This creates a routine for each student to prepare for learning, ease anxiety while practicing reading skills, and end each session on a positive note acknowledging the progress that has been made. Yoga strategies can be transferred to the home and classroom settings, thus improving outcomes for the learner as a whole.
